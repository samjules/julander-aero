About

Redefining Vertical Flight

Julander AERO is developing a new class of VTOL aircraft that combines the hover capability of ducted coaxial fan propulsion with the speed and range of a turboshaft-powered airframe. Our designs feature polished chrome fuselages, bubble canopies for maximum visibility, and thrust vectoring vane arrays for precise multi-axis control.

Compact VTOL Platform

The Type 610 personal hover vehicle features a streamlined fuselage at just 4.8m in length with a 2.2m ducted fan system, canted wing-skid landing gear, and an aero-duct skirt for ground effect operation.

  • 4.8m overall length, 3.5m skid span
  • Bubble canopy cockpit station
  • Canted wing-skid landing gear
  • Aero-duct skirt for ground effect

Ground Effect Lift Vehicle

The larger N61BJS concept features a 14.8m airframe with twin lift fan intakes, annular duct lift fan systems, short swept wings, and vectorable thrust nozzles for high-speed transition flight.

  • 14.8m overall length, 10.4m wingspan
  • Twin lift fan intakes with annular ducts
  • Variable pitch thrust vectors
  • Vectorable thrust nozzles at rear

Propulsion

Rolls-Royce Powered Ducted Coaxial Fan System

At the heart of every Julander AERO vehicle is a Rolls-Royce turboshaft engine driving counter-rotating coaxial fan stages through an epicyclic reduction gearbox. Thrust vectoring vane arrays beneath the duct provide omnidirectional control authority.
