Key Engineering Insights
Disc area advantage: NX7BWS has 88.4 m² total vs R66's 79.5 m² — an 11% raw advantage. With duct augmentation, effective disc area is ~104 m².
Power efficiency: Despite similar MTOW, NX7BWS requires significantly less power to hover than the R66 because of larger disc area (actuator disk theory — larger disc = lower induced velocity = less power).
Critical constraint: Empty weight fraction. Clamshell doors, retractable skid wings, and ducted skirt all add mass the R66 doesn't carry. Composite construction throughout is required.
CG zone: The fore-aft fan offset places the CG zone at the midpoint of the two fan centers (7.4m from nose). This must be respected in weight placement during loading.
Propulsion TBD: Power estimate of ~140–175 shp to hover means electric or hybrid propulsion is viable — well below R66-class turbine requirement.
